## Permanent lock

LayerCake remains byte-compatible at its external boundary:

```text
UTF-8 bytes in
UTF-8 bytes out
```

Its internal representation may use raw bytes, deterministic patches, a
versioned shared tokenizer, a token-plus-byte hybrid, or evidence-supported
dynamic latent patches. The production representation is selected only by a
controlled matched-quality bake-off. No representation receives a presumptive
win.

After prompt prefill, every continuation unit must be chosen from the frozen
model's neural output distribution by a declared decoding policy. Planners,
templates, forced next units, predetermined continuations, retrieval, stored
answers, copied corpus chunks, and deterministic response transducers are
prohibited.

One checkpoint must simultaneously establish quality, CPU speed, memory,
incremental execution, physical sparsity, generation behavior, and canonical
attachment-interface conformance. Phase 2 requires at least a 2x
optimized-transformer CPU throughput advantage, median latency no greater than
0.60x, no worse time to first useful output, lower active memory, and sustained
advantage through 1,024 generated bytes.

The representation bake-off holds raw corpora, data splits, hardware,
precision, training budgets, optimizer, quality suite, performance protocol,
and tuning allowance constant as far as possible. Tokenized branches are
compared under both fixed raw-byte exposure and fixed wall-clock or compute
exposure. Unavoidable differences are explicit. Promoted branches require at
least three independent seeds.

Portable cakes consume a versioned canonical semantic ABI rather than private
host token IDs. Compatibility may honestly be limited to one tokenizer class,
ABI version, and fusion contract. Cross-representation portability cannot be
claimed without identical conformance tests.

Phase 3 may begin only after the corrected Phase 2 release is committed, sealed
under `layercake-moonshot-phase2-r3`, and independently verified. Phase 3 must
preserve the exact Phase 2 representation and architecture and prove at least
2x lower wall-clock time to the same frozen general-quality threshold while
retaining at least 95% of certified Phase 2 CPU throughput.
